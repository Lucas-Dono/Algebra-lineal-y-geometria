# Proceso de Gram-Schmidt

El proceso de Gram-Schmidt transforma cualquier base en una base ortonormal.

## Idea

Dado un conjunto LI $\{\mathbf{v}_1, \ldots, \mathbf{v}_k\}$, construir un conjunto ortonormal $\{\mathbf{u}_1, \ldots, \mathbf{u}_k\}$ con el mismo generado.

La idea es:
1. Tomar el primer vector y normalizarlo
2. Para cada vector siguiente, restarle sus proyecciones sobre los anteriores y normalizar

## Algoritmo

**Entrada:** $\{\mathbf{v}_1, \ldots, \mathbf{v}_k\}$ linealmente independientes

**Proceso:**

**Paso 1:**
$$\mathbf{w}_1 = \mathbf{v}_1, \quad \mathbf{u}_1 = \frac{\mathbf{w}_1}{\|\mathbf{w}_1\|}$$

**Paso 2:**
$$\mathbf{w}_2 = \mathbf{v}_2 - \langle\mathbf{v}_2, \mathbf{u}_1\rangle\mathbf{u}_1, \quad \mathbf{u}_2 = \frac{\mathbf{w}_2}{\|\mathbf{w}_2\|}$$

**Paso 3:**
$$\mathbf{w}_3 = \mathbf{v}_3 - \langle\mathbf{v}_3, \mathbf{u}_1\rangle\mathbf{u}_1 - \langle\mathbf{v}_3, \mathbf{u}_2\rangle\mathbf{u}_2, \quad \mathbf{u}_3 = \frac{\mathbf{w}_3}{\|\mathbf{w}_3\|}$$

**Paso k (general):**
$$\mathbf{w}_k = \mathbf{v}_k - \sum_{j=1}^{k-1} \langle\mathbf{v}_k, \mathbf{u}_j\rangle\mathbf{u}_j, \quad \mathbf{u}_k = \frac{\mathbf{w}_k}{\|\mathbf{w}_k\|}$$

**Salida:** $\{\mathbf{u}_1, \ldots, \mathbf{u}_k\}$ ortonormal

## Ejemplo en R3

Ortonormalizar $\{\mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{v}_2, \mathbf{v}_3\}$ donde:
- $\mathbf{v}_1 = (1, 1, 1)$
- $\mathbf{v}_2 = (1, 1, 0)$
- $\mathbf{v}_3 = (1, 0, 0)$

**Paso 1:**
$$\|\mathbf{v}_1\| = \sqrt{3}$$
$$\mathbf{u}_1 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}(1, 1, 1)$$

**Paso 2:**
$$\langle\mathbf{v}_2, \mathbf{u}_1\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}(1 + 1 + 0) = \frac{2}{\sqrt{3}}$$

$$\mathbf{w}_2 = \mathbf{v}_2 - \frac{2}{\sqrt{3}} \cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}(1, 1, 1) = (1, 1, 0) - \frac{2}{3}(1, 1, 1)$$
$$= \left(\frac{1}{3}, \frac{1}{3}, -\frac{2}{3}\right)$$

$$\|\mathbf{w}_2\| = \sqrt{\frac{1}{9} + \frac{1}{9} + \frac{4}{9}} = \sqrt{\frac{6}{9}} = \frac{\sqrt{6}}{3}$$

$$\mathbf{u}_2 = \frac{3}{\sqrt{6}}\left(\frac{1}{3}, \frac{1}{3}, -\frac{2}{3}\right) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{6}}(1, 1, -2)$$

**Paso 3:**
$$\langle\mathbf{v}_3, \mathbf{u}_1\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}(1) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}$$

$$\langle\mathbf{v}_3, \mathbf{u}_2\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{6}}(1) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{6}}$$

$$\mathbf{w}_3 = (1, 0, 0) - \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}(1, 1, 1) - \frac{1}{\sqrt{6}} \cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{6}}(1, 1, -2)$$

$$= (1, 0, 0) - \frac{1}{3}(1, 1, 1) - \frac{1}{6}(1, 1, -2)$$

$$= \left(1 - \frac{1}{3} - \frac{1}{6}, -\frac{1}{3} - \frac{1}{6}, -\frac{1}{3} + \frac{2}{6}\right)$$

$$= \left(\frac{1}{2}, -\frac{1}{2}, 0\right)$$

$$\|\mathbf{w}_3\| = \sqrt{\frac{1}{4} + \frac{1}{4}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$$

$$\mathbf{u}_3 = \sqrt{2}\left(\frac{1}{2}, -\frac{1}{2}, 0\right) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(1, -1, 0)$$

**Resultado:**
- $\mathbf{u}_1 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}(1, 1, 1)$
- $\mathbf{u}_2 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{6}}(1, 1, -2)$
- $\mathbf{u}_3 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(1, -1, 0)$

## Verificacion

$$\langle\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{18}}(1 + 1 - 2) = 0$$
$$\langle\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_3\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{6}}(1 - 1 + 0) = 0$$
$$\langle\mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{12}}(1 - 1 + 0) = 0$$

Todos ortogonales y de norma 1.

## Descomposicion QR

El proceso de Gram-Schmidt produce la **descomposicion QR** de una matriz.

Si $A$ tiene columnas LI $\mathbf{a}_1, \ldots, \mathbf{a}_n$:

$$A = QR$$

donde:
- $Q$ es ortogonal (columnas son los $\mathbf{u}_i$)
- $R$ es triangular superior con entradas positivas en la diagonal

### Construccion

$$Q = \begin{pmatrix} | & | & & | \\ \mathbf{u}_1 & \mathbf{u}_2 & \cdots & \mathbf{u}_n \\ | & | & & | \end{pmatrix}$$

$$r_{ij} = \langle\mathbf{a}_j, \mathbf{u}_i\rangle \text{ para } i < j$$
$$r_{ii} = \|\mathbf{w}_i\|$$

### Aplicaciones de QR

1. Resolver sistemas $Ax = b$ de forma numericamente estable
2. Calcular autovalores (algoritmo QR)
3. Minimos cuadrados

## Gram-Schmidt en Espacios de Funciones

Ortonormalizar $\{1, x, x^2\}$ en $\mathcal{P}_2$ con $\langle f, g \rangle = \int_{-1}^{1} f(x)g(x)\,dx$.

**Paso 1:**
$$\|1\|^2 = \int_{-1}^{1} 1\,dx = 2$$
$$p_0(x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$$

**Paso 2:**
$$\langle x, p_0 \rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\int_{-1}^{1} x\,dx = 0$$ (funcion impar)

$$\|x\|^2 = \int_{-1}^{1} x^2\,dx = \frac{2}{3}$$

$$p_1(x) = \sqrt{\frac{3}{2}}x$$

**Paso 3:**
$$\langle x^2, p_0 \rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\int_{-1}^{1} x^2\,dx = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \cdot \frac{2}{3} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{3}$$

$$\langle x^2, p_1 \rangle = \sqrt{\frac{3}{2}}\int_{-1}^{1} x^3\,dx = 0$$

$$w_2(x) = x^2 - \frac{\sqrt{2}}{3} \cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} = x^2 - \frac{1}{3}$$

$$\|w_2\|^2 = \int_{-1}^{1} \left(x^2 - \frac{1}{3}\right)^2\,dx = \frac{8}{45}$$

$$p_2(x) = \sqrt{\frac{45}{8}}\left(x^2 - \frac{1}{3}\right)$$

Estos son (multiplos de) los **polinomios de Legendre**.
